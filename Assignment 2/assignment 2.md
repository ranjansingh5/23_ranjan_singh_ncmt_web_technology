# 5. â€œPlanning is the most critical phase of web development.â€ Discuss by listing key activities.â“
-> Planning is considered the most critical phase because it defines the direction of the entire project. Mistakes made here can affect all later stages.

* Key activities during the planning phase include:

- Requirement analysis: Understanding what the client or users need from the website.
- Goal definition: Deciding the purpose of the website (information, sales, services, etc.).
- Target audience identification: Knowing who will use the website.
- Sitemap creation: Planning the structure and navigation of pages.
- Technology selection: Choosing tools, languages, and platforms.
- Timeline and resource planning: Estimating cost, time, and team roles.
- Proper planning reduces errors, saves time, and ensures smooth development.

# 6. Explain the iterative nature of the Maintenance phase. Why is a website never truly â€œfinishedâ€?â“
-> The Maintenance phase is iterative because it involves continuous updates and improvements even after the website is launched.

* Reasons why a website is never truly finished:
- User needs and expectations keep changing.
- Content needs regular updates (news, products, information).
- Bugs and technical issues may appear over time.
- Security updates and performance improvements are required.
- New technologies and design trends emerge.
Because these changes happen repeatedly, maintenance follows a cycle of monitoring, updating, testing, and improving.

# 8. You are the project manager for a new e-commerce site. The client wants to skip the â€œTestingâ€ phase to save money and launch early. Write a persuasive argument explaining the potential risks.â“
-> As a project manager, I would explain to the client that skipping the Testing phase can create serious risks that may harm the business in the long run.

First, there are security risks. Without proper testing, security loopholes such as weak login systems or insecure payment gateways may remain unnoticed. This can lead to customer data breaches and financial loss.
Second, functionality issues may occur. Important features like product search, cart, checkout, or payment confirmation may not work correctly, causing customers to abandon purchases.
Third, there are compatibility problems. The website may work on one browser or device but fail on others, leading to poor user experience for many customers.

In conclusion, testing ensures the website is secure, reliable, and works smoothly on all devices. Skipping this phase may save money initially but can cause bigger losses and damage to the brand after launch.
